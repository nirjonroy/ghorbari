Dhaka Apartment Prices: What Buyers Should Watch This Year

Understand demand, location premiums, and practical signals before you make an offer in Bangladesh's busiest housing market.

Land Site Editorial June 28, 2026 6 min read

Dhaka apartment prices continue to move differently by neighborhood, road access, building age, and handover readiness. Buyers should compare total cost, not only the asking price.

Location Still Drives The Premium

Areas such as Gulshan, Banani, Dhanmondi, Bashundhara R/A, and Uttara remain highly competitive because they combine access, schools, offices, hospitals, and lifestyle services. A slightly higher price can be reasonable when commute savings and resale value are stronger.

Compare Price Per Square Foot

Instead of comparing only total price, buyers should review price per square foot, parking value, utility readiness, service charges, and expected renovation costs. Two apartments with similar asking prices can have very different real value.

A clean ownership file, practical floor plan, and reliable building management can matter as much as the headline price.

What Buyers Should Check

  • Developer reputation and delivery history.
  • RAJUK approval, mutation status, and ownership documents.
  • Road width, drainage, parking access, and lift backup.
  • Monthly service charge and building maintenance quality.
  • Recent transaction prices in the same neighborhood.

Practical Buying Strategy

Shortlist three to five comparable properties before making an offer. Use inspection notes, recent listings, and document readiness to negotiate confidently. If a property is priced higher than nearby options, ask what justifies the premium.
